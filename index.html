Kurt Vonnegut, Jr.

Laughing in the face of death

Vonnegut wrote 14 novels

Timeline of Vonnegut's Life:

  • 1922 - Kurt was born on November 11th in Indianapolis.
  • 1940 - Kurt graduated from Shortridge High School and matriculated at Cornell University.
  • 1943 - Kurt dropped out of Cornell and enlisted in the Army
  • 1944 - Kurt's mother Edith committed suicide and he was deployed to the Battle of the Bulge, where he was captured and sent to a POW camp in Dresden.
  • 1945 - Kurt returned to the states, married Jane Cox (a high school sweetheart) and began work as a publicist for General Electric.
  • 1952 - Kurt's first novel Player Piano was published and received positive reviews.
  • 1959 - Kurt published his second novel Sirens of Titan and third Mother Night in 1961.
  • 1963 - Vonnegut published Cat's Cradle, his favorite of his own novels.
  • 1969 - Kurt published Slaughterhouse-Five, probably his magnum opus and most critically succesful novel.
  • 2007 - Vonnegut passed away in Mahattan on April 11th.
